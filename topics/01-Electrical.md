# Topic 1: Basic Electrical & Electronics Engineering

> **50 Questions** | Direct Answers | Cheat Sheet Format

---

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is Ohm's Law?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> V = IR (Voltage = Current × Resistance)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What are the SI units for Voltage, Current, Resistance?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Voltage = Volt (V), Current = Ampere (A), Resistance = Ohm (Ω)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: In series circuit, how do voltages add?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> V_total = V1 + V2 + V3 + ... (Series addition)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: In parallel circuit, how do currents add?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> I_total = I1 + I2 + I3 + ... (Parallel addition)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is terminal voltage formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> V_terminal = ε - Ir (ε = EMF, r = internal resistance)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: State Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL).</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> ΣV = 0 around any closed loop</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: State Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL).</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> ΣI_in = ΣI_out at any node</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Delta to Star conversion formula for equal resistance R.</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> R_Y = R_Δ / 3</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: State Superposition Theorem.</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Response = Sum of responses due to each source acting alone</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: State Thevenin's Theorem.</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Any linear 2-terminal circuit = V_th in series with R_th</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: State Norton's Theorem.</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Any linear 2-terminal circuit = I_N in parallel with R_N</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Maximum Power Transfer Theorem condition?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> R_load = R_Thevenin (Maximum power when impedances match)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is resonance in RLC circuit?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> X_L = X_C (Inductive reactance = Capacitive reactance), Z = R (minimum)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Resonant frequency formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> f₀ = 1/(2π√LC)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is power factor?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> PF = cos(φ) = Real Power / Apparent Power</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Active Power formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> P = VI cos(φ) [Watts]</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Reactive Power formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Q = VI sin(φ) [VAR - Volt-Ampere Reactive]</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Apparent Power formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> S = VI [VA - Volt-Ampere]</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: In 3-phase AC system, phase difference between phases?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> 120° (or 2π/3 radians)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: 3-phase voltage vs single-phase?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> V_3phase = √3 × V_single-phase</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is diode forward bias?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Positive to anode, negative to cathode. Conducts with ~0.7V drop (silicon)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is diode reverse bias?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Negative to anode, positive to cathode. Blocks current (OFF)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor)?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> 2 PN junctions (NPN or PNP). Terminals: Base (B), Collector (C), Emitter (E)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: BJT Common Emitter configuration characteristic?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> High voltage gain, high current gain, most used configuration</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is MOSFET?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor FET. Uses SiO₂ insulating layer. High input impedance, low power</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is CMOS technology?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Complementary MOS (NMOS + PMOS). One ON, one OFF. Very low static power consumption</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is impedance?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Z = √(R² + (X_L - X_C)²) [Total AC opposition to current]</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: RC time constant τ formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> τ = R × C [seconds]</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Capacitor charging formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> V_C(t) = V(1 - e^(-t/τ))</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Capacitor discharging formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> V_C(t) = V × e^(-t/τ)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is transformer?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Transfers energy between circuits via EM induction. AC only. Turns ratio = V₁/V₂ = n₁/n₂</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: RMS value vs Peak value?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> V_RMS = V_peak / √2 ≈ 0.707 × V_peak</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Example: 230V AC rating is what peak value?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Peak = 230 × √2 ≈ 325V</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is oscillator?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Circuit generating periodic signal. Types: RC, LC, Crystal</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Crystal oscillator frequency stability?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Highly stable, ppm-level accuracy. Used in clocks, communications</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is feedback? Types?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Positive: Reinforces input (increases gain, reduces stability). Negative: Opposes input (decreases gain, improves stability)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Negative feedback benefits?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Reduces distortion, improves linearity, reduces noise, increases bandwidth, improves stability</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is op-amp (Operational Amplifier)?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> High-gain DC amplifier with feedback. Ideal: Infinite gain, infinite input Z, zero output Z</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Common op-amp ICs?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> 741 (basic), TL072 (high performance), LM358 (low cost)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Class A amplifier efficiency?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> ~25%. High distortion. Single transistor. Conducts full 360°</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Class B amplifier efficiency?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> ~78.5%. Crossover distortion. Two transistors. Each conducts 180°</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Class AB amplifier efficiency?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> ~60-70%. Compromise A & B. Reduced crossover distortion. Two transistors with bias</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Decibel (dB) formula for power?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> dB = 10 log₁₀(P₂/P₁)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Decibel (dB) formula for voltage?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> dB = 20 log₁₀(V₂/V₁)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What does 3dB point mean?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Half-power point. Voltage ratio = 1.414 (√2)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is common-emitter amplifier voltage gain formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> A_v = -g_m × R_c = -βR_c/r_e (negative = 180° phase inversion)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Mutual inductance formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> V₂ = -M(dI₁/dt)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Coupling coefficient formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> k = M / √(L₁L₂) [Range: 0 to 1]</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Q-factor (Quality factor) formula?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Q = ω₀L/R = 1/(ω₀RC) = f₀/BW</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Low-pass filter cutoff frequency?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> f_c = 1/(2πRC)</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Open-loop vs Closed-loop gain in op-amp?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Open-loop: 10⁴-10⁶ (very high, unstable). Closed-loop: A_cl ≈ 1/β (stable, predictable)</blockquote>
</details>
