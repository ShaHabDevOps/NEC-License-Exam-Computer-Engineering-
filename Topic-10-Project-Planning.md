<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is a project?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Temporary endeavor with defined start/end. Produces unique output. Specific objectives</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Project life cycle phases?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Initiation → Planning → Execution → Monitoring/Control → Closure</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Project Charter?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Authorizes project. Contains objectives, scope, constraints, stakeholders</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Project Scope?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> What is INCLUDED and EXCLUDED. Defines boundaries</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: GANTT Chart?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Bar chart showing schedule. Tasks, start/end dates, duration</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Critical Path Method (CPM)?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Identifies longest task sequence. Determines minimum project duration</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Slack/Float in project?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Time activity can delay without delaying project. Critical path activities = zero slack</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: PERT (Program Evaluation Review Technique)?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Estimates duration: (Optimistic + 4×Most likely + Pessimistic) / 6</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Resource leveling?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Balances resource allocation. Reduces conflicts</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Risk management process?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Identify → Analyze → Plan response → Monitor</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What is a contingent project?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Depends on outcome of another project</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Earned Value Management (EVM)?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Tracks progress: planned value vs earned value vs actual cost</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Orthographic projection?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> 2D representation. Front, top, side views. Parallel lines stay parallel</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Isometric projection?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> 3D appearance. 30° angles. Shows three dimensions</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: NPV (Net Present Value)?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Sum of discounted cash flows. >0: Project viable</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: IRR (Internal Rate of Return)?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Discount rate where NPV = 0. Higher IRR = better investment</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Payback Period?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Time to recover initial investment. Quick but ignores time value</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Professional Ethics in Engineering?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Honesty, safety, sustainability, public welfare above personal gain</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: What does NEA stand for?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Nepal Engineering Association</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Occupational Health and Safety?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> Responsibility of engineers to ensure safe working conditions</blockquote>
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q: Regulatory environment in Nepal?</b></summary>
<blockquote><b>Answer:</b> NEC (Nepal Engineering Council) regulates engineering practices and licenses engineers</blockquote>
</details>
